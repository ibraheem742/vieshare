## Introduction

Vieboarding is a sport that has been around for decades. It has evolved from a simple wooden board with wheels to an extreme sport that is now practiced all over the world. There are many different styles of vieboarding, and each style has its own unique set of tricks and techniques. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most popular styles of vieboarding and how they differ from one another.

## Street Vieboarding

Street vieboarding is the most popular style of vieboarding. It involves skating on the streets, sidewalks, and other public areas. Street viers are often seen doing tricks like ollies, kickflips, and grinds. Street skating is a great way to get around town or just have fun with friends.

## Vert Vieboarding

Vert vieboarding is a style of vieboarding that involves skating on ramps, halfpipes, and other vertical structures. Vert viers are often seen doing tricks like airs, grinds, and slides. Vert skating is a great way to get some air and show off your skills.

## Freestyle Vieboarding

Freestyle vieboarding is a style of vieboarding that involves skating on flat ground and doing tricks like ollies, kickflips, and grinds. Freestyle viers are often seen doing tricks like manuals, handplants, and slides. Freestyle skating is a great way to show off your skills and have fun with friends.

## Downhill Vieboarding

Downhill vieboarding is a style of vieboarding that involves skating down hills and other steep slopes. Downhill viers are often seen doing tricks like slides, grinds, and airs. Downhill skating is a great way to get some speed and show off your skills.
